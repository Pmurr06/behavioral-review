For Authors

We're excited to work with you. Whether you're submitting for the first time or you're an experienced writer, these guidelines will walk you through our process and what we're looking for.

What We Accept

We accept submissions in the following categories:

Criminal Case Analysis

In-depth analysis of criminal cases, investigative approaches, and evidence evaluation.

Behavioral Analysis

Analysis of human behavior, behavioral patterns, and decision-making in various contexts.

Missing Persons Analysis

Research and analysis related to missing persons cases and investigative approaches.

Investigative Commentary

Evidence-based commentary on criminal investigation, forensic practices, and investigative methods.

Research Papers

Original empirical research and studies in criminology, psychology, or behavioral science.

Literature Reviews

Comprehensive reviews of existing research on specific topics in our focus areas.

Criminology Editorials

Student-authored editorials on criminology topics, trends, and current issues.

Psychology Editorials

Student-authored editorials on psychology topics, research developments, and applications.

Submission Guidelines

📝 Manuscript Length

1,500–5,000 words (including references)

📚 Citation Style

Use APA, MLA, or Chicago style. Choose whichever you're most comfortable with. Just be consistent throughout your paper.

🔗 Sources

Use credible, properly cited sources. We focus on quality over quantity. Credible sources include:

  • Peer-reviewed academic journals
  • Books by recognized scholars
  • Government reports and databases
  • Professional publications
  • Reputable news sources for current events

No source requirement—what matters is that your sources are credible and properly cited.

✍️ Formatting

Submit your work as a Word document (.docx) or PDF. Use a standard font (Times New Roman, Arial, or Georgia) in 12-point size, with 1-inch margins on all sides.

📌 Title Page

Include a title page with:

  • Manuscript title
  • Your name and email
  • Your university affiliation
  • Word count
  • Submission category

Can I Submit a Class Paper?

Yes! You can submit a revised and expanded class paper as long as:

  • ✓ You have significantly revised and improved it
  • ✓ You have expanded it with additional research or analysis
  • ✓ You have properly cited all sources
  • ✓ It still fits within our 1,500–5,000 word range
  • ✓ You have permission from your original instructor if required by your school

A thoughtful class paper is a great foundation for publication. We'll help you polish it into a piece you can be proud of.

Our Editorial Review Process

Here's exactly what happens when you submit your manuscript.

1

Submission Received by Managing Editor

Your manuscript is received, logged, and reviewed for completeness and scope fit.

2

Manuscript Logged and Anonymized

The Managing Editor logs your submission and removes all author identifying information for blind peer review.

3

Section Editor Review

The anonymized paper is sent to the appropriate Section Editor who provides detailed editorial feedback.

4

Editorial Feedback and Revision

You receive feedback and have the opportunity to revise and resubmit your manuscript.

5

Copy & Layout Editor Review

Our Copy & Layout Editor reviews formatting, grammar, clarity, citation consistency, AI-assisted content screening, and publication readiness.

6

Managing Editor Final Approval

The Managing Editor makes the final approval decision and determines publication readiness.

7

Publication

Your article is published online, added to our archive, and featured in Recent Articles.

What We Look For

💡 Clear Thinking

Your ideas should be well-developed and easy to follow. Show your reasoning.

🔍 Strong Analysis

Go beyond summarizing. Analyze, interpret, and apply what you've learned.

📖 Credible Sources

Use reliable sources and cite them properly. Show your research.

✍️ Professional Tone

Write at an academic level. Proofread carefully. First impressions matter.

🎯 Clear Purpose

Your reader should understand what you're arguing or analyzing from the start.

🤝 Ethical Research

Original work, proper attribution, and ethical treatment of topics.

Ready to Submit?

Send your manuscript to contact@thebehavioralreview.com with the subject line "Manuscript Submission — [Your Title]". Include your completed title page and manuscript as a single attachment in .docx or PDF format.

Questions before submitting? Visit our Contact page — our editors are happy to discuss your work.

Questions?

How long does the review process take?

Typically 2–4 weeks from submission to final decision, depending on our workload. We'll keep you updated.

What if my work is rejected?

We'll provide feedback. Many authors revise and resubmit—we're here to help you improve.

Can I revise and resubmit after rejection?

Absolutely. We encourage you to take the feedback and make improvements, then resubmit.

Do I keep the rights to my work?

Yes, you retain all rights to your published work. We simply have permission to publish it.

What if I have questions while writing?

Reach out! Our editors are happy to discuss your ideas before you submit.

Will I be anonymous during review?

Your manuscript will be anonymized for our Section Editor's review to ensure fair, unbiased feedback.
