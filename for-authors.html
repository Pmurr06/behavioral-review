Submission guide

For Authors

Everything you need to prepare, format, and submit undergraduate research to The Behavioral Review.

Submission checklist

Ready to Submit

  • Currently enrolled undergraduate author (individual or co-authored work)
  • 1,500–5,000 words including references
  • Original work with APA 7 citations and references throughout
  • Clear academic argument with evidence-based analysis
  • Use subject line: "Manuscript Submission — [Your Name]"
  • Email your manuscript to contact@thebehavioralreview.com
Accepted work

What We Publish

  • Original Research — empirical and analytical studies with clear methods and findings
  • Literature Reviews — structured syntheses that identify patterns and research gaps
  • Case Analyses — real-world events examined through behavioral or social science frameworks
  • Commentary & Policy Analysis — argument-driven pieces on policy questions with academic grounding
What happens next

Editorial Review Process

Most decisions are issued within 2–4 weeks.

  1. 1

    Initial Check & Blind Review

    The Managing Editor confirms scope and removes identifying details. A Section Editor then evaluates the manuscript and provides editorial feedback.

  2. 2

    Revision & Copy Review

    You may revise and resubmit. Copy and layout review checks clarity, formatting, and citation consistency before a final decision.

  3. 3

    Publication

    Accepted manuscripts are published online and added to the permanent archive.

Frequently asked questions

FAQs

Can I submit a revised class paper?

Yes—revised class papers are welcome when they are substantially improved, clearly argued, and fully cited.

Can multiple authors submit together?

Yes—co-authored manuscripts are accepted when all contributors are identified at submission.

Can I submit to another journal while under review?

No—simultaneous submissions are not permitted while your manuscript is under consideration. You may submit elsewhere only after withdrawal or a final decision.

How long does the review process take?

Typically 2–4 weeks. The editorial team will keep you updated throughout.
