About The Behavioral Review

Our Mission

The Behavioral Review was founded to give undergraduate students a place to publish original research and build a portfolio they can use for graduate school, law school, internships, and careers. We provide an editor-reviewed platform where student researchers can share work in behavioral science, criminology, psychology, and criminal justice.

We believe undergraduate research represents meaningful intellectual work. Student scholars deserve an outlet for that work — and constructive editorial feedback that helps them grow.

About This Publication

The Behavioral Review is a newly launched undergraduate publication. We are currently accepting submissions and building our inaugural collection of student research. We are transparent about where we are: a small, committed editorial team with a clear mission and room to grow.

We do not claim institutional affiliations or faculty oversight that do not exist. What we offer is an honest editorial process, constructive feedback, and a permanent online home for your published work.

Editorial Philosophy

We publish original research, case analyses, investigative commentary, and evidence-based academic work that advances understanding of human behavior within criminal justice and the broader social sciences. Our editors provide constructive feedback that challenges authors to strengthen their work while keeping the process accessible for undergraduate contributors.

For the full list of accepted submission categories, visit the For Authors page.

Editorial Standards

Originality

We publish original research, analysis, and commentary not previously published elsewhere.

Evidence-Based Reasoning

All work is evaluated for methodological soundness and intellectual substance.

Relevance

Articles must address meaningful questions in behavioral science, criminology, or criminal justice.

Clarity

Writing must be clear, well-organized, and accessible to an educated undergraduate audience.

Fair Review

Editorial review is conducted by section editors who provide constructive feedback to authors.

Author Rights

Authors retain all rights to their published work. We have permission to publish and archive it.
