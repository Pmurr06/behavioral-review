About The Behavioral Review

Our Mission

The Behavioral Review was founded to help undergraduate students transform classroom knowledge into published research while building a professional portfolio for graduate school, law school, internships, and future careers. We provide a professional editor-reviewed platform where student researchers can publish rigorous work in behavioral science, criminology, psychology, and criminal justice.

We believe that undergraduate research represents meaningful intellectual contribution and that student scholars deserve a professional outlet for their work. Our publication fosters academic excellence by maintaining rigorous editorial standards while supporting student development as scholars.

Editorial Philosophy

The Behavioral Review publishes original research, case analyses, investigative commentary, and evidence-based academic work that advances understanding of human behavior within the context of criminal justice systems and the broader social sciences.

We emphasize methodological rigor, theoretical sophistication, and practical relevance. Our editors provide constructive editorial feedback that challenges authors to strengthen their work while maintaining an accessible tone for undergraduate-level publication.

We Welcome Submissions In:

  • Criminology: Theories of crime, crime patterns, criminal behavior, victimology, and criminological research
  • Psychology & Criminal Behavior: Psychological profiles, offender assessment, trauma, mental health in criminal justice contexts
  • Behavioral Analysis: Decision-making, human behavior patterns, investigative psychology, threat assessment
  • Criminal Justice Systems: Policy analysis, institutional reform, sentencing, rehabilitation, reintegration
  • Criminal Case Analysis: High-profile case examinations, investigative commentary, forensic interpretation

Who Reads The Behavioral Review

The Behavioral Review reaches an audience of undergraduate students, graduate students, faculty, practitioners in criminal justice and mental health fields, and professionals in law enforcement, corrections, and legal services. Our publication provides a platform where student research reaches meaningful audiences and contributes to professional discourse.

Contributors benefit from publication in an editor-reviewed publication, professional editorial guidance, and a polished final product suitable for graduate applications, professional portfolios, and career advancement.

About This Publication

The Behavioral Review is a newly established editor-reviewed undergraduate publication founded with a vision to elevate student academic work and provide student researchers with a professional publication outlet. Like established academic publications, The Behavioral Review combines rigorous editorial standards with mentorship of emerging scholars.

The publication is currently accepting submissions and building its inaugural collection of student research in behavioral science and criminal justice.

Editorial Standards

All submissions to The Behavioral Review undergo rigorous editorial review according to academic standards. The publication maintains the following principles:

Originality

We publish original research, analysis, and commentary not previously published elsewhere.

Rigor

All work is evaluated for methodological soundness, evidence-based reasoning, and intellectual substance.

Relevance

Articles must address significant questions in behavioral science, criminology, or criminal justice.

Clarity

Writing must be clear, well-organized, and accessible to an educated undergraduate audience.

Fair Editorial Review

Editorial review is conducted by qualified section editors who provide constructive feedback to authors.

Professionalism

All interactions maintain professional standards in keeping with academic publishing norms.
