Artificial Intelligence and Public Perception: A Rhetorical Analysis of Competing Perspectives

Ella Roehl and Christine Byrne

University of Washington

Public Policy July 2026 10 min read

Introduction

Artificial intelligence (AI) has rapidly become one of the most debated technological developments of the twenty-first century. Although AI research has existed for decades, advances in large language models and generative AI have dramatically increased public awareness, prompting widespread discussions about the technology’s ethical, social, and economic implications. Supporters argue that AI has the potential to accelerate innovation, improve productivity, and expand access to information, while critics warn that it may contribute to misinformation, bias, job displacement, and insufficient regulatory oversight.

These competing perspectives have shaped public conversations surrounding AI, influencing how individuals, organizations, and policymakers understand both its opportunities and its risks. Media organizations, nonprofit groups, and technology publications each frame artificial intelligence differently, emphasizing particular benefits or concerns depending on their intended audiences and institutional goals. As a result, public perception of AI is often influenced as much by rhetorical presentation as by the technology itself.

This article analyzes three sources that present contrasting perspectives on artificial intelligence: the Inventiv Foundation’s discussion of ChatGPT as a tool for innovation, The New York Times’ reporting on calls for a temporary pause in advanced AI development, and Liberties’ critique of the societal risks associated with artificial intelligence. By examining each source’s language, organization, evidence, and intended audience, this analysis explores how rhetorical choices shape public understanding of one of the most significant technological debates of the modern era.

Optimism and Innovation: The Inventiv Foundation

The first source examined is the Inventiv Foundation article, “How ChatGPT Can Help Individual Inventors?” Rather than framing artificial intelligence as a threat, the article presents AI as a practical tool capable of accelerating creativity, research, and innovation. Its primary audience consists of inventors, entrepreneurs, and individuals interested in developing new ideas more efficiently. Throughout the article, the author argues that AI can reduce many of the time and resource constraints that traditionally slow the invention process, allowing users to generate ideas, conduct research, and refine designs more quickly.

The article establishes an optimistic tone immediately through both its visual presentation and language. A lightbulb logo, commonly associated with innovation and creativity, reinforces the article’s emphasis on technological progress. This message is further supported by imagery depicting raised hands, symbolizing collaboration, participation, and the generation of new ideas. Together, these visual elements encourage readers to associate artificial intelligence with opportunity and advancement before engaging with the written content.

The author’s word choice further strengthens this positive framing. Terms such as “game changer,” “revolutionized,” “effective tool,” and “powerful” present AI as a transformative technology capable of improving numerous aspects of the invention process. Rather than relying solely on emotional appeal, however, the article also incorporates practical examples, explaining how ChatGPT can assist with research, idea generation, and written communication. These examples reinforce the author’s broader argument that AI should be viewed as a resource that complements, rather than replaces, human creativity.

Although the overall message is favorable toward artificial intelligence, the article does not ignore its limitations. Individual sections discussing applications such as manufacturing, marketing, and product development conclude by acknowledging potential weaknesses, including bias, plagiarism, inaccurate responses, and the absence of human experience and critical judgment. The author notes that AI-generated writing lacks the emotional depth and nuanced reasoning that characterize human communication while also cautioning readers about responsible use in academic and professional settings.

This balanced presentation strengthens the article’s credibility. By acknowledging both the advantages and shortcomings of artificial intelligence, the Inventiv Foundation avoids presenting AI as either entirely beneficial or entirely harmful. Instead, the article argues that the technology’s value depends largely on how it is applied. This measured approach encourages readers to view artificial intelligence as a tool requiring responsible oversight rather than as an inherently positive or negative force.

Caution and Regulation: The New York Times

The second source examined is The New York Times article, “Elon Musk and Others Call for Pause on A.I., Citing ‘Profound Risks to Society.’” Unlike the Inventiv Foundation’s optimistic framing, this article presents artificial intelligence as a technology requiring caution, oversight, and public discussion. Rather than advocating for the abandonment of AI, the article focuses on growing concerns among technology leaders and researchers who argue that rapid AI development should be accompanied by stronger regulation and responsible governance.

The article establishes credibility through both its publication and the individuals it features. The headline prominently references Elon Musk, one of the world’s most recognizable technology entrepreneurs, immediately signaling authority and drawing readers’ attention. While Musk’s perspective serves as the article’s focal point, the authors broaden the discussion by including the concerns of other researchers and industry leaders who argue that increasingly powerful AI systems present significant societal risks if developed without appropriate safeguards.

Although the article emphasizes caution, it does not portray artificial intelligence as inherently harmful. Instead, the authors acknowledge the remarkable progress AI has achieved through advances in large language models, explaining how systems such as ChatGPT have become increasingly capable as they are trained on expanding datasets (Metz & Schmidt, 2023). By presenting both technological achievements and potential risks, the article encourages readers to view AI as a powerful innovation that also requires careful oversight.

The credibility of the article is further strengthened by its authors, Cade Metz and Gregory Schmidt. Metz has written extensively on artificial intelligence and emerging technologies, including The Genius Makers, while Schmidt regularly reports on current events for The New York Times. Their professional backgrounds reinforce the article’s informative tone, which relies on reporting expert opinions rather than persuading readers toward a predetermined conclusion.

Unlike sources that strongly advocate either for or against artificial intelligence, The New York Times primarily presents a policy-oriented discussion. The article frames AI as a developing technology whose future depends on responsible governance, public accountability, and informed decision-making. This rhetorical approach encourages readers to weigh competing perspectives rather than accept a single definitive conclusion, making the article appear balanced and credible despite addressing a highly controversial topic.

Fear and Persuasion: Liberties

The final source examined is the Liberties article, “7 Disadvantages of Artificial Intelligence Everyone Should Know About.” Unlike the previous two sources, this article adopts a distinctly cautionary tone, presenting artificial intelligence primarily through the lens of its potential harms. Rather than balancing benefits and drawbacks, the article seeks to persuade readers that AI poses significant risks to society, emphasizing concerns such as unemployment, algorithmic bias, misinformation, environmental impacts, and the growing influence of large technology companies.

The article’s structure reinforces this persuasive objective. By organizing its discussion around seven clearly defined disadvantages, the author presents AI as a collection of interconnected societal threats rather than a technology with competing strengths and weaknesses. This straightforward format guides readers toward viewing the technology primarily through its risks, creating a cumulative effect in which each section reinforces the overall argument.

Language also plays an important rhetorical role. Throughout the article, the author employs words with strongly negative connotations, describing artificial intelligence as “faulty,” “riddled with errors,” and “poorly cleansed.” These descriptions encourage readers to associate AI with unreliability and danger before evaluating the supporting evidence. Statistical claims, including projections that millions of workers may need to transition into new occupations because of automation (Arena, 2022), further strengthen the article’s appeal by combining emotional concern with quantitative evidence.

The credibility of the article is also shaped by its institutional context. Published by Liberties, an organization dedicated to protecting civil and human rights throughout Europe, the article approaches artificial intelligence from the perspective of its potential impact on society, democracy, and individual rights. This mission naturally influences the issues the publication chooses to emphasize, leading it to focus more heavily on ethical concerns than on technological innovation.

Compared with the previous two sources, the Liberties article presents the least balanced rhetorical approach. While the Inventiv Foundation acknowledges limitations despite its optimistic perspective and The New York Times presents competing viewpoints surrounding AI regulation, Liberties concentrates almost exclusively on the technology’s potential dangers. This selective emphasis is itself an important rhetorical strategy, illustrating how an author’s purpose and intended audience influence which evidence is highlighted and which perspectives receive less attention.

Comparing the Three Perspectives

Although each source examines artificial intelligence, they differ significantly in how they frame the technology and the future it represents. These differences demonstrate that public understanding of AI is shaped not only by the facts presented but also by the rhetorical strategies authors use to communicate those facts. Through language, organization, source selection, and intended audience, each article encourages readers to adopt a distinct perspective on artificial intelligence and its role in society.

The Inventiv Foundation approaches AI primarily as a tool for innovation and productivity. While acknowledging limitations such as bias and plagiarism, the article consistently emphasizes the opportunities artificial intelligence offers to inventors and entrepreneurs. Its optimistic tone encourages readers to view AI as a resource that can enhance human creativity when used responsibly.

In contrast, The New York Times frames artificial intelligence as a rapidly evolving technology that warrants careful oversight. Rather than arguing for or against AI itself, the article focuses on the broader policy questions surrounding regulation, accountability, and responsible development. By presenting the perspectives of technology leaders and researchers alongside evidence of AI’s capabilities, the article invites readers to consider both the promise and the potential risks of continued advancement.

The Liberties article adopts the most persuasive and cautionary position. Instead of presenting competing viewpoints, it emphasizes the societal consequences of artificial intelligence, including unemployment, algorithmic bias, misinformation, and the growing influence of large technology companies. This selective emphasis reflects the publication’s mission of advocating for civil and human rights while encouraging readers to approach AI with skepticism.

Taken together, these three sources illustrate that discussions surrounding artificial intelligence are not simply technological debates but also rhetorical ones. The same emerging technology can be portrayed as an opportunity for innovation, a policy challenge requiring thoughtful regulation, or a societal threat demanding immediate attention. Understanding these competing narratives allows readers to evaluate AI more critically by recognizing how rhetorical choices influence public perception, policy discussions, and attitudes toward technological change. Rather than accepting any single perspective at face value, audiences benefit from examining the evidence, purpose, and intended audience behind each argument before forming their own conclusions.

Conclusion

The debate surrounding artificial intelligence extends far beyond the technology itself. As the three sources examined demonstrate, public understanding of AI is shaped by the rhetorical choices authors make regarding language, evidence, organization, and intended audience. Whether AI is presented as an engine of innovation, a policy issue requiring regulation, or a societal threat depends largely on how each source constructs its argument and frames the technology’s potential consequences.

The Inventiv Foundation emphasizes opportunity and technological progress while acknowledging practical limitations. The New York Times presents AI through the lens of governance and responsible development, encouraging readers to consider both innovation and regulation. In contrast, Liberties adopts a predominantly cautionary perspective, highlighting ethical concerns and the potential social consequences of widespread AI adoption. Together, these articles demonstrate that discussions surrounding artificial intelligence are shaped not only by technological developments but also by differing institutional priorities and rhetorical strategies.

Analyzing these contrasting perspectives reinforces the importance of critical media literacy when evaluating emerging technologies. Artificial intelligence continues to evolve rapidly, and public opinion will increasingly influence decisions regarding its regulation, implementation, and ethical use. Readers should therefore examine not only the claims being made about AI but also the evidence supporting those claims, the motivations of the authors, and the audiences each source seeks to persuade.

Ultimately, the future impact of artificial intelligence will depend not solely on advances in technology but also on the quality of public discourse surrounding it. By approaching competing perspectives with careful analysis rather than immediate acceptance or rejection, individuals are better equipped to participate in informed conversations about one of the most significant public policy challenges of the modern era.
