Our Editorial Review Process

1

Student Submission

Authors submit manuscripts through our online portal with supporting materials and author information.

→
2

Initial Editorial Review

Managing Editor assesses scope, originality, and compliance with submission guidelines.

→
3

Section Editor Review

Subject-matter editor provides detailed editorial feedback and recommendations.

4

Revision (if needed)

Authors address feedback and resubmit revised manuscript for further evaluation.

→
5

Managing Editor Approval

Final editorial decision and acceptance determination made by Managing Editor.

→
6

Publication

Accepted articles are copyedited, formatted, and published in the archive.

Our Mission

The Behavioral Review was founded to help undergraduate students transform classroom knowledge into published scholarship while building a professional portfolio for graduate school, law school, internships, and future careers. We provide a professional editor-reviewed platform where student researchers can publish rigorous work in behavioral science, criminology, psychology, and criminal justice.
