Editorial Team

Managing Editor

Paulo Murray

Founder & Managing Editor

Paulo Murray is the founder and Managing Editor of The Behavioral Review. He is responsible for the overall vision, strategic direction, and editorial decision-making for the publication. Paulo oversees manuscript evaluation, manages the editorial team, and ensures The Behavioral Review maintains rigorous editorial standards and professionalism.

Section Editors

Our Section Editors bring expertise and leadership to their respective areas. They conduct editorial review, provide author guidance, and shape the intellectual direction of their sections.

Criminology Editor

Section Editor

Oversees submission and review of criminological research, including crime theories, criminal behavior analysis, victimology, and criminological methodology. Evaluates empirical studies, theoretical frameworks, and policy-relevant research in the criminology discipline.

Behavioral Analysis Editor

Section Editor

Manages submissions related to behavioral analysis, investigative psychology, decision-making patterns, threat assessment, and applied behavioral science. Focuses on evidence-based approaches to understanding human behavior in forensic and criminal justice contexts.

Production Team

Our production team ensures that accepted manuscripts are professionally edited, properly formatted, and published to academic standards.

Copy & Layout Editor

Production Editor

Responsible for copyediting, style consistency, formatting, citation accuracy, and page layout of published articles. Ensures all published work meets professional publishing standards and maintains visual and textual consistency across the publication.

Professor Ambassador Program

The Professor Ambassador Program invites faculty mentors at universities to champion The Behavioral Review among their students and departments. Professor Ambassadors serve as liaisons between the publication and their institutions, helping students identify research opportunities and encouraging submissions.

Professor Ambassador Benefits:

  • Direct communication channel with The Behavioral Review editorial office
  • Early notification of editorial initiatives and special issues
  • Recognition in publication materials and on the website
  • Opportunity to recommend students and manuscripts
  • Collaboration on workshops and student training

Interested in becoming a Professor Ambassador? Please contact us for more information.

Our Commitment to Editorial Excellence

Intellectual Integrity

We maintain rigorous standards for scholarship and evidence-based reasoning, ensuring all published work meets academic expectations.

Student Support

We provide constructive feedback and mentorship to emerging scholars, helping students develop as researchers and writers.

Professional Standards

Our editorial processes follow established academic publishing norms and best practices in scholarly communication.

Diversity of Perspective

We welcome submissions from diverse educational backgrounds and perspectives that contribute to understanding of behavioral science and criminal justice.
